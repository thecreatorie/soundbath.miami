01 · Posture

The page is a calling card.

Maximum restraint. We give little. We ask carefully.

Soundbath is exclusive by posture, not by language. The brand should feel like a closed door with a small bell — people who get the URL already know, or are curious enough to introduce themselves to find out.

Every surface obeys the same logic: the page asks, the email answers. Communications are deliberate, infrequent, and confident. Nothing is sold. Nothing is hyped.

DEFAULT RULE

"What would the deck do?"

When in doubt, defer to the deck. Every surface inherits the deck's restraint, type discipline, and white space.

02 · Voice

We sound like ourselves.

Calm

Sentences end. We don't pile clauses. We don't apologize for taking space.

Specific

Names, neighborhoods, instruments, hours. We trade abstractions for concrete things people can picture.

Direct

"Introduce yourself." Not "Welcome to our community." We address one person at a time.

House lines

"A music-driven, community-led bathhouse."
"This is where wellness meets wildness."
"Introduce yourself."
"Received."
"— Soundbath"

Vermilion on oxblood is the brand's home key. Cream on oxblood is the reading default. Vermilion on cream is reserved for high-emphasis moments (the wordmark, accents, buttons against light backgrounds).

Never

No gradients. No tints. No transparency on color blocks. No drop shadows. No "warm" or "cool" filters on photography. Color is binary — either it is one of the four, or it isn't on the brand.

05 · Wordmark

The wordmark holds the room.

Set in Fraunces, full caps, edge-to-edge of its container. Typography carries everything — there is no companion mark.
Primary · Vermilion on Oxblood
Light · Vermilion on Cream
Bold · Cream on Vermilion
Sizing

The wordmark always fills the available width edge-to-edge. There is no minimum size, no maximum size. Use SVG and let it scale. Letterspacing is set by the font — do not add tracking.

Clearspace

Negative space is the clearspace. The wordmark needs no padding zone — it asks for the whole frame and the layout makes room.

07 · Forms

Every field is a question we'd ask a guest at dinner.

Forms earn their length by asking carefully. The form is the only thing the page does — it can be substantive when it's the only thing.
Field anatomy

Label: Mono caps, 11px, 0.12em tracking, white.
Input: Transparent background, 1px white-line border-bottom only.
Focus: Border-bottom transitions to vermilion, 200ms.
Error: Vermilion mono, lowercase, below the field.
Required mark: Vermilion asterisk, never a "(required)" label.

Submit button

Default: Vermilion fill, white text, sharp corners, mono caps.
Hover: Inverted — white fill, vermilion text.
Loading: Button text becomes SENDING…
Post-submit: Replace form in place with a Fraunces italic confirmation: Received. — Soundbath

08 · Don'ts

What we never say. What we never make.

Verbal — never use

  • "Spa" / "wellness platform" / "thermal wellness experience"
  • "Biohacking" / "optimize"
  • "Exclusive" / "luxury" / "VIP"
  • "Where sound meets stillness"
  • "Founding circle" / "founding member" badges
  • "Limited spots" / countdowns
  • "Welcome!" / "We're so excited"
  • FAQ language, social proof, press logos
spa biohacking optimize luxury VIP exclusive thermal wellness where sound meets stillness founding circle limited spots

Visual — never make

  • Rounded corners, anywhere
  • Drop shadows, glows, or blurs
  • Gradients or tinted overlays
  • Stock wellness imagery (candles, robes, pebbles)
  • Hero CTA buttons or scroll cues
  • Three or more typefaces
  • Decorative dividers, ornaments, or icons
  • Color outside the four-token palette
We give little.
We ask carefully.
