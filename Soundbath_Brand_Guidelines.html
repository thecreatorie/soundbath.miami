01
Brand Essence
Soundbath is a thermal wellness experience coming to Miami. The brand should feel like an invitation to something that already carries weight — exclusive, natural, unhurried, and quietly counter-cultural.
Positioning

A silent counter culture revolution in wellness

Soundbath sits at the intersection of thermal wellness, sound healing, and curated human connection. It is not a spa, not a gym, not a members’ club with a wellness floor. It is a new category — a platform for presence.

We Are

Exclusive but not elitist. Natural but not rustic. Modern but not cold. Sensory but not overwhelming. Confident but never loud.

We Are Not

A tech startup. A wellness trend. A social media experience. Performative. Cheap. Rushed. Mainstream. A place where phones are welcome.

Core Pillars

Thermal Wellness · Sound Healing · Music Experiences · Community · Plant Medicine Ceremonies

Target Feeling

The feeling of stepping into warm water in a beautiful, dark, quiet room — where you can hear your own breathing and the sound of something ancient and modern happening at the same time.

Usage Rules

DO Use Burnt Orange only on CTAs, labels, and small text accents
DON'T Use Burnt Orange as a background fill for any section
DO Alternate between Warm White and Charcoal Earth sections for rhythm
DON'T Use pure black (#000) or pure white (#FFF) anywhere
DO Use Sage Mineral sparingly — image overlays, hover states, secondary moments
DON'T Combine Burnt Orange and Sage Mineral in the same element

04
Voice & Tone
Soundbath speaks like someone who doesn’t need to convince you. The tone is confident, warm, and spare. Every word earns its place.

Confident, Not Loud

We state what we are without defending it. No exclamation marks. No superlatives. The restraint communicates luxury more than any adjective could.

Warm, Not Friendly

The tone has warmth but not casualness. We don’t say “hey there!” or “we’re so excited.” We speak like a host welcoming you into a beautiful room.

Mysterious, Not Vague

We reveal just enough. The mystery is intentional — it creates pull. But when we do share something, it’s specific and vivid, never filler.

Counter-Cultural, Not Preachy

We position against the status quo without attacking it. “No phones. No performative wellness.” Let people draw their own conclusions about what that means.

This sounds like us “A thermal wellness experience built around sound, water, and the kind of human connection Miami has been missing.”
This does not “Welcome to Soundbath! We’re building an amazing new wellness community in Miami and we can’t wait for you to experience it!”
This sounds like us “You’re one of them now.”
This does not “Thanks for signing up! You’re officially part of the Soundbath fam!”
05
Photography Direction
Until original photography exists, source stock or reference images that match these moods. When shooting original content, follow these principles.
Warm stone
Steam & shadow
Amber glow
Still water
Natural texture
Heat & light

Use

Close-up textures: stone, water, steam, wood grain. Warm amber and candlelight tones. Abstract and sensory. People shown from behind or partially — the space is the subject, not the individual. References: Therme Vals, Aman resorts, Japanese onsen photography.

Avoid

Bright, clinical spa lighting. Stock photos of people in white robes smiling. Overhead flat-lays. Anything that looks like a fitness brand, a tech startup, or a generic wellness Instagram. No phones visible in any image, ever.
